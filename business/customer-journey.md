# Customer Journey Map for Consent-Guardrails

## Aware Phase

### Trigger Event
* Government officials, policymakers, and advocacy groups become aware of the need for standardized consent and data protection frameworks in AI and technology.
* Industry reports, news articles, or regulatory updates highlight the importance of consent and data protection.

### Friction Points
* Lack of clear guidelines and regulations for consent and data protection.
* Limited resources and expertise to implement and maintain effective consent and data protection frameworks.

### User Emotions
* Concerned about the potential risks and consequences of non-compliance.
* Frustrated by the complexity and lack of clarity in existing regulations.

### Opportunities to Delight
* Provide easy-to-understand guidelines and resources for implementing consent and data protection frameworks.
* Offer expert advice and support for navigating complex regulations.

### Metric
* Number of government officials, policymakers, and advocacy groups aware of the need for standardized consent and data protection frameworks (Awareness Rate).

## Consider Phase

### Trigger Event
* Government officials, policymakers, and advocacy groups begin to consider implementing consent and data protection frameworks in AI and technology.
* They start to research and evaluate different options and solutions.

### Friction Points
* Difficulty in finding a solution that meets their specific needs and requirements.
* Concerns about the cost and feasibility of implementing a consent and data protection framework.

### User Emotions
* Uncertain about the best course of action.
* Worried about the potential costs and implications of implementing a consent and data protection framework.

### Opportunities to Delight
* Provide clear and concise information about the benefits and features of Consent-Guardrails.
* Offer personalized support and guidance to help them make an informed decision.

### Metric
* Number of government officials, policymakers, and advocacy groups considering implementing consent and data protection frameworks (Consideration Rate).

## Try Phase

### Trigger Event
* Government officials, policymakers, and advocacy groups decide to try out Consent-Guardrails.
* They sign up for a free trial or demo to test the platform.

### Friction Points
* Difficulty in navigating the platform and understanding its features.
* Concerns about the effectiveness and reliability of the platform.

### User Emotions
* Excited to try out a new solution.
* Frustrated by the complexity and difficulty of using the platform.

### Opportunities to Delight
* Provide clear and concise onboarding and training materials.
* Offer dedicated support and resources to help them get started and overcome any challenges.

### Metric
* Number of government officials, policymakers, and advocacy groups who complete a free trial or demo (Activation Rate).
